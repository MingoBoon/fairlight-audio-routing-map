Reference · plain English

The glossary

Every acronym and term used in the toolkit, explained for someone brand new to audio mixing. The same definitions appear as hover tooltips on the interactive map.

The sections — what the letters mean
DX
Dialogue — all spoken voice.
VO
Voice-Over — narration recorded in a booth, separate from filmed dialogue.
FX / SFX
(Sound) Effects — doors, cars, whooshes, hits.
PFX
Production Effects — non-speech sounds the dialogue microphones caught on location (footsteps, door slams). Kept with dialogue because they share its recording acoustics; usually excluded from the M&E and replaced with library sounds in a dub.
Futz
Voice deliberately degraded to sound like a phone, radio or TV speaker.
Ambience / BG
Backgrounds — continuous atmosphere such as room tone, wind, traffic.
MX
Music.
M&E
Music & Effects — the entire mix minus dialogue. Foreign broadcasters replace the speech with their own language, so they need everything else ready-made in one file. Usually a mandatory deliverable.
Signal path
Track
A lane in the timeline that holds audio clips. Has its own volume fader, pan, EQ and effects. See one on the map →
Bus
A meeting point that sums several signals into one — think "group channel". Stems, reverbs, LFE collectors and masters are all buses; only their routing differs. In modern Resolve's FlexBus system, all buses are created identically and the role comes from routing.
Stem
A bus used as a category submix (all dialogue, all FX…). Controlled by one master fader and also exported as its own file. See the DX Stem →
Print Master
The final mix bus — everything summed. What you monitor and deliver. ("Print" is old film language for recording the final result.) See it on the map →
Send (Bus Send)
A tap that siphons an adjustable copy of a signal to another bus (e.g. to a reverb). The original keeps flowing unaffected.
Return
The processed (wet) signal coming back from a reverb bus into a stem.
Aux (Auxiliary)
The traditional name for a bus you reach via sends, like the toolkit's reverb buses.
Insert
A processor placed directly in a channel's path, affecting everything through it (unlike a send, which is a parallel copy).
Fader
The volume slider of a track or bus.
−∞ (minus infinity)
A fader or send pulled all the way down: silence. The template's sends are pre-wired at −∞ so projects open clean.
VCA
Voltage Controlled Amplifier (a historic mixing-desk term). In Resolve: a fader that remote-controls other faders (level, mute, solo). No audio passes through it; members keep their own routing. Up to 16 groups via Fairlight ▸ VCA Assign. See one on the map →
Pan / Panner
Positions a sound between speakers. The surround panner places it anywhere in the 5.1 field.
Pre / post-fader
Whether a send taps the signal before or after the fader. Post (the default) means the echo follows the fader down.
Automation
Recorded fader/knob moves that replay with the timeline.
Patching
Connecting signals to physical inputs/outputs (microphones in, speakers out). Fairlight ▸ Patch Input/Output.
Channels & formats
Mono / Stereo
One channel / two channels (Left + Right).
5.1
Six channels: Left, Right, Centre, LFE (subwoofer), Left surround, Right surround. The "5" are full-range speakers; the ".1" is the limited-range bass channel.
5.0
5.1 without the LFE channel — used for the surround reverbs, since echo doesn't belong in the subwoofer.
LFE
Low Frequency Effects — the ".1": a dedicated deep-bass channel for the subwoofer. See the FX LFE bus →
Ls / Rs
Left surround / Right surround — the rear speakers.
SMPTE order
The standard channel order in delivered files: L R C LFE Ls Rs. (Film order differs: L C R Ls Rs LFE. Deliver SMPTE unless the spec says otherwise.)
Downmix / fold-down
Automatically converting a surround mix to stereo.
Lt/Rt vs Lo/Ro
Two stereo flavours. Lt/Rt ("total") encodes the surround information so a decoder can rebuild it; Lo/Ro ("only") is a plain stereo sum. Your broadcaster's delivery spec says which one they want.
Bass management
A playback system automatically re-routing low frequencies to the subwoofer. The template's LFE buses exist so you control the sub deliberately instead.
48 kHz / 24-bit
Professional sample rate and bit depth for film/TV audio files.
Loudness & delivery
LUFS / LKFS
Loudness Units relative to Full Scale — the standard measure of perceived loudness. LKFS is the identical US name. More negative = quieter (−24 is quieter than −14).
Integrated
The single loudness number for the whole programme; what delivery specs are judged on.
Short-term / Momentary
Loudness over the last 3 seconds / 400 milliseconds — for watching in the moment while you mix.
LRA (Loudness Range)
How much the loudness varies across the programme. Broadcast tolerates less range than cinema.
dBTP / true peak
Decibels True Peak — the real maximum level after digital-to-analogue conversion. Specs cap it (e.g. −2 dBTP) to prevent distortion on consumer equipment.
Dialog-gated
Loudness measured only while speech is present — how Netflix-style streaming services measure. A narration-heavy programme reads very differently this way, which is why one master can't satisfy both broadcast and streaming.
EBU R128
European broadcast loudness standard: −23 LUFS integrated, −1 dBTP.
ATSC A/85 · CALM Act
US broadcast loudness standard (−24 LKFS, −2 dBTP) and the US law that enforces it.
Deliverable
Any file the client or broadcaster contractually requires: masters, stems, M&E, etc.
WAV / AAC / MP3
WAV is the uncompressed professional file format. AAC/MP3 are compressed web formats (used only for the Webmix here).
.drp
A DaVinci Resolve Project export file — how you save the template for reuse (File ▸ Export Project).
Processing
EQ (Equaliser)
Boosts or cuts chosen frequency ranges — tone control.
Compressor
Automatically turns down loud moments, evening out the level. The ratio (e.g. 3:1) sets how firmly.
Limiter
A brick-wall compressor that stops the signal exceeding a ceiling — the last safety net before delivery.
De-esser
Tames harsh "S" sounds in a voice.
Reverb
Simulates a space's echo — room, plate, hall. See a reverb bus →
Low-pass filter
Lets frequencies below a cutoff through and removes the rest. ~120 Hz on the LFE buses keeps only sub-bass.
Chain FX
Resolve 21 feature: several plugins saved together as one reusable preset (up to 6 per chain).
FairlightFX
Resolve's built-in suite of audio plugins (Reverb, Compressor, Limiter, EQ, etc.).
