🐣 New to mixing? Start here

This whole template is a river system. Your audio clips sit on tracks (the lanes of the timeline). Each track flows into a stem — a submix that gathers one category together: all the talking in one place (DX), all the sound effects in another (FX), all the music in a third (MX). The stems then flow into the Print Master — the finished mix you actually listen to and export.

Why bother with the middle layer? Three reasons:

  • Control: one fader turns all dialogue up or down at once.
  • Deliverables: broadcasters want the mix and the separated stems as files, so it can be re-edited or dubbed later.
  • The M&E: a version of the mix with no dialogue, needed so foreign broadcasters can dub in their own language. It's built automatically from the FX and MX stems.

Riding alongside the river: VCAs (remote-control volume knobs that move several faders at once — no audio flows through them), reverb buses (shared echo machines many tracks can borrow), and LFE buses (collectors for deep bass that feed the subwoofer).

How to read the map: audio flows left → right. Click any box for what it is + how to build it; click any line for what that connection does. Dotted-underlined words anywhere show a definition when you hover. Full glossary below.

📖 Glossary — every acronym & term
The sections (what the letters mean)
DX
Dialogue — all spoken voice.
VO
Voice-Over — narration recorded in a booth, separate from filmed dialogue.
FX / SFX
(Sound) Effects — doors, cars, whooshes, hits.
PFX
Production Effects — non-speech sounds the dialogue microphones caught on location (footsteps, door slams). Kept with dialogue because they share its sound.
Futz
Voice deliberately degraded to sound like a phone, radio or TV.
Ambience / BG
Backgrounds — continuous atmosphere like room tone, wind, traffic.
MX
Music.
M&E
Music & Effects — the entire mix minus dialogue. Foreign broadcasters replace the speech with their own language, so they need everything else in one file. Usually a mandatory deliverable.
Signal path
Track
A lane in the timeline that holds audio clips. Has its own volume fader, pan, EQ and effects.
Bus
A meeting point that sums several signals into one. Think "group channel". Stems, reverbs, LFE collectors and masters are all buses — only their routing differs.
Stem
A bus used as a category submix (all dialogue, all FX…). Also exported as its own file.
Print Master
The final mix bus — everything summed. What you monitor and deliver. ("Print" is old film language for recording the final result.)
Send
A tap that siphons an adjustable copy of a signal to another bus (e.g. to a reverb). The original keeps flowing unaffected.
Return
The processed (wet) signal coming back from a reverb bus into a stem.
Aux
Auxiliary — the traditional name for a bus you reach via sends, like our reverb buses.
Insert
A processor placed directly in a channel's path, affecting everything through it (unlike a send, which is a parallel copy).
Fader
The volume slider of a track or bus.
−∞ (minus infinity)
A fader or send pulled all the way down: silence. Our template's sends are pre-wired at −∞ so projects open clean.
VCA
Voltage Controlled Amplifier — a historic mixing-desk term. In Resolve: a fader that remote-controls other faders (level, mute, solo). No audio passes through it.
Pan / Panner
Positions a sound between speakers. The surround panner places it anywhere in the 5.1 field.
Pre / post-fader
Whether a send taps the signal before or after the fader. Post (default) means the echo follows the fader down.
Automation
Recorded fader/knob moves that replay with the timeline.
Patching
Connecting signals to physical inputs/outputs (mics in, speakers out).
Channels & formats
Mono / Stereo
One channel / two channels (Left + Right).
5.1
Six channels: Left, Right, Centre, LFE (subwoofer), Left surround, Right surround. The "5" are full-range speakers; the ".1" is the limited-range bass channel.
5.0
5.1 without the LFE channel — used for our surround reverbs, since echo doesn't belong in the subwoofer.
LFE
Low Frequency Effects — the ".1": a dedicated deep-bass channel for the subwoofer.
Ls / Rs
Left surround / Right surround — the rear speakers.
SMPTE order
The standard channel order in delivered files: L R C LFE Ls Rs. (Film order differs: L C R Ls Rs LFE.)
Downmix / fold-down
Automatically converting a surround mix to stereo.
Lt/Rt vs Lo/Ro
Two stereo flavours: Lt/Rt ("total") encodes the surround info so it can be decoded back; Lo/Ro ("only") is a plain stereo sum. Your broadcaster's spec says which.
Bass management
A playback system automatically re-routing low frequencies to the subwoofer. Our LFE buses exist so we control the sub deliberately instead.
48 kHz / 24-bit
Professional sample rate and bit depth for film/TV audio files.
Loudness & delivery
LUFS / LKFS
Loudness Units relative to Full Scale — the standard measure of perceived loudness. LKFS is the identical US name. More negative = quieter (−24 is quieter than −14).
Integrated
The single loudness number for the whole programme — what specs are judged on.
Short-term / Momentary
Loudness over the last 3 s / 400 ms — for watching in the moment.
LRA
Loudness Range — how much the loudness varies. Broadcast tolerates less range than cinema.
dBTP / true peak
Decibels True Peak — the real maximum after digital-to-analogue conversion. Specs cap it (e.g. −2 dBTP) to prevent distortion on consumer gear.
Dialog-gated
Loudness measured only while speech is present — how Netflix-style services measure. Narration-heavy shows read very differently this way.
EBU R128
European broadcast loudness standard: −23 LUFS.
ATSC A/85 · CALM
US broadcast standard (−24 LKFS) and the US law that enforces it.
Deliverable
Any file the client/broadcaster contractually requires: masters, stems, M&E…
WAV / AAC / MP3
WAV = uncompressed professional file. AAC/MP3 = compressed web formats (Webmix only).
.drp
A DaVinci Resolve Project export file — how you save this template for reuse.
Processing
EQ (Equaliser)
Boosts or cuts chosen frequency ranges — tone control.
Compressor
Automatically turns down loud moments, evening out the level. Ratio (e.g. 3:1) sets how firmly.
Limiter
A brick-wall compressor that stops the signal exceeding a ceiling — the last safety net.
De-esser
Tames harsh "S" sounds in a voice.
Reverb
Simulates a space's echo — room, hall, plate.
Low-pass filter
Lets frequencies below a cutoff through, removes the rest. ~120 Hz on our LFE buses keeps only sub-bass.
Chain FX
Resolve 21 feature: several plugins saved together as one reusable preset.
Resolve 21 version notes — read once (FlexBus)

Bussing changed in v17 and this map's "Main / Sub / Aux" labels are roles, not bus types. Modern Resolve uses FlexBus: every bus is created the same way (Fairlight ▸ Bus Format ▸ Add Bus — set name, format, colour) and its role comes from routing:

  • "Sub" (stems): a bus that track Bus Outputs feed into.
  • "Aux" (reverbs): a bus reached only via Bus Sends; its own output returns to a stem.
  • "Main" (print masters): the last bus in the chain, monitored and delivered.

FlexBus limits: a track can output to up to 10 buses and send to 10 more; buses can feed other buses up to 6 layers deep. The legacy fixed Main/Sub/Aux topology still exists (Project Settings ▸ Fairlight ▸ "Use fixed bus mapping", new empty projects only; converting old projects to FlexBus is one-way) — don't use it for new work.

VCAs are real: up to 16 groups via Fairlight ▸ VCA Assign (or the Mixer's Group dropdown). VCA masters appear to the right of the buses in the Mixer.

Free vs Studio: buses, VCAs, 5.1, FairlightFX and loudness metering all work in the free version. Studio adds immersive formats (5.1.2–9.1.6 / Atmos — enable in Preferences ▸ Video and Audio I/O ▸ Immersive Audio), Dolby Digital encoding, and the AI voice tools.

New since this template was specced:

  • v20: AI Audio Assistant (auto first-pass mix — fine for temp mixes, not a replacement for this routing), AI Dialogue Separator & Dialogue Matcher, Voice Isolation, full mixer + loudness metering on the Cut page.
  • v21: audio track folders (collapse the 11 source tracks into Dial / FX / Amb / Mus groups), 6-band Clip EQ, Chain FX presets (save the Webmix comp+limiter as one preset, up to 6 FX), EQ Match / Level Matcher clip processing, new Fairlight Audio Core (higher track/plugin counts). 21.0.1 / 21.0.2 are maintenance releases — no Fairlight routing changes.

Export: 48 kHz / 24-bit; 5.1 SMPTE channel order L R C LFE Ls Rs (film order is L C R Ls Rs LFE — deliver SMPTE unless the spec says otherwise).

Build order — quick checklist
  1. Project: new project, Project Settings ▸ Fairlight ▸ 48 kHz sample rate; leave "Use fixed bus mapping" off (FlexBus).
  2. Buses first: Fairlight ▸ Bus Format ▸ Add Bus × 21. The Format column is where you decide mono / stereo / 5.1 for each bus — set it exactly:
    BusFormat
    5.1 Print Master5.1
    Stereo Print Master · Webmix StereoStereo
    DX / FX / MX / M&E Stem5.1 (×4)
    Stereo DX / FX / MX / M&E StemStereo (×4)
    FX LFE Bus · Music LFE BusMono (becomes the .1 feed)
    DX Verb ×3 · FX Verb ×3Mono / Stereo / 5.0
    MX Verb ×2Stereo / 5.0
    Exact names matter: they appear in every routing dropdown. (Reverbs are 5.0, not 5.1 — no echo in the subwoofer.)
  3. Bus-to-bus routing: Fairlight ▸ Bus Assign — stems → print masters; FX/MX stems also → M&E; LFE buses → their stems (panned 100% to LFE); reverbs → their stems; Stereo PM → Webmix. M&E gets no onward routing.
  4. Tracks: add the 11 source tracks with correct widths, rename, colour-code. (v21 tip: group them into track folders per section.)
  5. Track outputs: each track → its 5.1 stem and stereo stem (Mixer Bus Outputs +, or Alt/Option-click for multi-assign).
  6. Panning: dialogue hard Centre; FX across the field, Centre clear; ambience wide into surrounds; music L/R or discrete 5.1.
  7. LFE sends: FX + Music tracks → Bus Sends → LFE buses; low-pass ~120 Hz on each LFE bus.
  8. Reverbs: FairlightFX Reverb on each verb bus; pre-route track sends at −∞.
  9. VCAs: Fairlight ▸ VCA Assign — 4 groups (Dialogue / FX / Ambience / Music).
  10. Monitoring + meters: monitor the 5.1 PM (Control Room); loudness meters on both print masters — pick the standard in the meter options; use dialog-gated mode for the streaming check.
  11. Webmix: Compressor + Limiter on the Webmix bus (~3:1, ceiling −1 dBTP, ≈ −14 LUFS). Save as a Chain FX preset.
  12. Save template: File ▸ Export Project (.drp), keep as master template.
