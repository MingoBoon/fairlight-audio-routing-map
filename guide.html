Build the Fairlight 5.1 template, step by step

Everything you need to recreate a professional 5.1 film/TV mixing template in DaVinci Resolve — written for people who are new to audio mixing. Use this page side-by-side with the interactive routing map: every element below links straight to its node on the map.

1 · New to mixing? Start here

This whole template is a river system. Your audio clips sit on tracks (the lanes of the timeline). Each track flows into a stem — a submix that gathers one category together: all the talking in one place (DX), all the sound effects in another (FX), all the music in a third (MX). The stems then flow into the Print Master — the finished mix you actually listen to and export.

11 SOURCE TRACKS → STEMS (submixes) → PRINT MASTERS (final mixes) → monitoring / delivery
                     ↑                     ↓
               reverbs (aux)          Webmix (compressed stereo)

Why bother with the middle layer?

  • Control: one fader turns all dialogue up or down at once.
  • Deliverables: broadcasters want the mix and the separated stems as files, so the programme can be re-edited or dubbed later.
  • The M&E: a version of the mix with no dialogue, needed so foreign broadcasters can dub in their own language. It builds itself automatically from the FX and MX stems.

Riding alongside the river: VCAs (remote-control volume knobs that move several faders at once — no audio flows through them), reverb buses (shared echo machines many tracks can borrow), and LFE buses (collectors for deep bass that feed the subwoofer).

2 · Resolve 21 version notes (read once)

Bussing changed in v17 — "Main / Sub / Aux" are roles, not bus types. Modern Resolve uses FlexBus: every bus is created the same way (Fairlight ▸ Bus Format ▸ Add Bus — name, format, colour) and its role comes from routing: stems receive track outputs, reverbs receive Bus Sends, masters are the last bus in the chain.
  • FlexBus limits: a track can output to up to 10 buses and send to 10 more; buses feed other buses up to 6 layers deep.
  • Legacy fixed bussing still exists (Project Settings ▸ Fairlight ▸ "Use fixed bus mapping", new empty projects only, one-way conversion back) — don't use it for new work.
  • VCAs: up to 16 groups via Fairlight ▸ VCA Assign; masters appear right of the buses in the Mixer.
  • Free vs Studio: buses, VCAs, 5.1 and loudness metering all work in the free version. Studio adds immersive formats (Atmos), Dolby Digital encoding and the AI voice tools.
  • New in v20/21: AI Audio Assistant (temp mixes only — it doesn't replace this routing), Dialogue Matcher, Voice Isolation (v20); audio track folders, 6-band Clip EQ, Chain FX presets, EQ/Level Match (v21). 21.0.1/21.0.2 are maintenance releases.

5 · Elements in depth

Each group below matches a column on the map — the "view on map" links open the map with that element selected and its full signal path lit up.

Source tracks view on map ↗

In plain English: a track is a lane in the timeline where you drop audio clips. Everything on the lane flows wherever its output points — into its family's stem.

  • Narration/VO carries an information piece: own fader, de-esser + light compression, no reverb. Streaming loudness is dialog-gated, so this track largely decides whether you pass the −27 LKFS check.
  • Dialogue lives hard Centre — anchored to the screen for every seat, and it folds down predictably.
  • Futz = deliberately degraded voice (phone/radio/TV). Do the degradation with clip FX — v21's 6-band Clip EQ is ideal.
  • PFX stays with dialogue (shared recording acoustics); the dub replaces it with library FX.
  • Ambience folds into the FX stem — standard for backgrounds and M&E. Need a separate BG deliverable? Add a BG Stem later.
  • Music (5.1) passes channel-for-channel; its LFE send is for reinforcing stereo-sourced cues.
VCAs — group volume control view on map ↗

In plain English: a remote-control volume knob. Pull it down and every member track turns down together, keeping their relative balance. No sound travels through it.

Build: Fairlight ▸ VCA Assign ▸ create and name each group ▸ tick member tracks. Masters appear right of the buses in the Mixer; members keep their own routing.

Why: one-fader control of a whole section without collapsing it to a bus — per-track automation, panning and stem routing stay untouched. This is your top-level balance layer.

Reverb buses (aux role) view on map ↗

In plain English: a shared echo machine. Tracks tip a little sound in (a "send"); it comes out wet and is added back into the submix (the "return"). One reverb serves many tracks at different amounts.

Build each: Bus Format ▸ Add Bus (Mono/Stereo/5.0) ▸ Mixer ▸ Effects ▸ FairlightFX ▸ Reverb, 100% wet ▸ Bus Outputs ▸ + ▸ its stem. Feed it only via Bus Sends.

Why: returning the verb into the matching stem keeps wet signal inside the correct deliverable — dialogue tails can never pollute the M&E. Presets: room/plate for mono/stereo, hall/scoring for 5.0.

LFE buses — the ".1" view on map ↗

In plain English: a collector for deep bass. Tracks tip low rumble in, a ~120 Hz low-pass keeps only sub-bass, and the output is panned 100% into the stem's LFE channel — so you decide what shakes the room, not the playback system's bass management.

Separate FX and Music LFE buses mean the M&E and mix stems each carry their own correct low end.

Stems (sub role) & the M&E view on map ↗

In plain English: a stem is a submix — all of one category in one place with one master fader, also exported as its own file so the mix can be taken apart and re-versioned later.

The M&E trick: M&E is fed bus-to-bus from the FX and MX stems, never from tracks. That guarantees it always equals FX + Music + Ambience with dialogue removed — change any level and the M&E follows automatically. Leave its output unassigned; it's a deliverable, not part of the monitored mix. M&E is usually a mandatory deliverable.

Print Masters & Webmix view on map ↗

In plain English: the finished mix — every stem summed. What you listen to, what the loudness meters judge, what gets exported.

  • 5.1 Print Master: monitor it via the Control Room; loudness meters colour-code against your chosen standard (blue in-spec / yellow tolerance / red over); switch to dialog-gated for streaming checks. Deliver as 6-ch WAV (L R C LFE Ls Rs) or Dolby Digital (Studio).
  • Stereo Print Master: a discrete stereo mix (not just a fold-down); set Lt/Rt or Lo/Ro per spec; meter it separately.
  • Webmix: Compressor (~3:1) + Limiter (−1 dBTP ceiling) — save as one Chain FX preset — landing ≈ −14 LUFS for web platforms.

6 · The routes, explained

Every line on the map is one of eight connection types. Click any line on the map for the same info in context.

Track output → bus

The track's post-fader, post-pan signal is mixed into the bus — the main path from a lane into its stem. Create: Mixer ▸ Bus Outputs ▸ + (Alt/Option-click for many tracks at once), or Fairlight ▸ Bus Assign.

Bus Send → reverb (aux send)

A copy of the sound is siphoned to the echo machine; the original keeps flowing unaffected. Create: Mixer ▸ Bus Sends ▸ + ▸ the verb bus; right-click the send for pre/post-fader (post is typical). Template default: −∞.

Reverb return → stem

The echo joins the group mix, so the echo of dialogue always lives with the dialogue. Create: the verb bus's own Bus Outputs ▸ + ▸ the stem.

LFE send → LFE bus

Some of the track's deep bass is siphoned to the bass collector. Create: Bus Sends ▸ + ▸ the LFE bus. Filtering happens once, on the bus.

LFE bus → stem's LFE channel

The collected, filtered bass is poured into the subwoofer channel. Create: Bus Outputs ▸ + ▸ the stem, then pan the bus's output 100% to LFE.

VCA assignment (control only)

No sound — a volume remote-control link (dashed red on the map). Create: Fairlight ▸ VCA Assign, or the Mixer Group dropdown.

Stem → M&E

The effects/music group is also copied into the "no dialogue" version, automatically. Create: Bus Assign ▸ the stem's Out ▸ also tick M&E.

Bus → master chain

The group mix is poured into the final mix (stems → print masters; Stereo PM → Webmix). Create: Bus Assign or Bus Outputs ▸ +.

Premium streaming (Netflix-style)−27 LKFS−2 dBTPdialog-gated

No single master satisfies all four — broadcast measures the whole programme while streaming measures only where speech is present. Mix once, then produce normalized versions. Always verify against each recipient's current spec sheet.

Export from the Deliver page — each bus renders as its own file, 48 kHz / 24-bit, SMPTE order L R C LFE Ls Rs: the 5.1 Print Master (6-ch WAV or Dolby Digital), all stems including the mandatory M&E, the Stereo Print Master (Lt/Rt or Lo/Ro per spec), stereo stems, and the Webmix as AAC/MP3.

8 · Glossary

The sections
DX
Dialogue — all spoken voice.
VO
Voice-Over — narration recorded in a booth, separate from filmed dialogue.
FX / SFX
(Sound) Effects — doors, cars, whooshes, hits.
PFX
Production Effects — non-speech sounds the dialogue mics caught on location. Kept with dialogue; excluded from the M&E.
Futz
Voice deliberately degraded to sound like a phone, radio or TV speaker.
Ambience / BG
Backgrounds — continuous atmosphere such as room tone, wind, traffic.
MX
Music.
M&E
Music & Effects — the entire mix minus dialogue, for foreign-language dubbing. Usually mandatory.
Signal path
Track
A lane in the timeline that holds audio clips, with its own fader, pan, EQ and effects.
Bus
A meeting point that sums several signals into one — a "group channel". In FlexBus all buses are created identically; the role comes from routing.
Stem
A bus used as a category submix, also exported as its own file.
Print Master
The final mix bus — what you monitor and deliver.
Send (Bus Send)
A tap that siphons an adjustable copy of a signal to another bus; the original is unaffected.
Return
The processed (wet) signal coming back from a reverb bus into a stem.
Aux
The traditional name for a bus you reach via sends.
Insert
A processor placed directly in a channel's path, affecting everything through it.
Fader
The volume slider of a track or bus.
−∞ (minus infinity)
A fader or send pulled all the way down: silence. Template sends are pre-wired at −∞.
VCA
Voltage Controlled Amplifier (historic desk term) — a fader that remote-controls other faders. No audio passes through it. Up to 16 groups via Fairlight ▸ VCA Assign.
Pan / Panner
Positions a sound between speakers; the surround panner places it anywhere in the 5.1 field.
Pre / post-fader
Whether a send taps the signal before or after the fader. Post (default) means the echo follows the fader down.
Automation
Recorded fader/knob moves that replay with the timeline.
Patching
Connecting signals to physical inputs/outputs (mics in, speakers out).
Channels & formats
Mono / Stereo
One channel / two channels (Left + Right).
5.1
Six channels: Left, Right, Centre, LFE (subwoofer), Left surround, Right surround.
5.0
5.1 without the LFE channel — used for surround reverbs.
LFE
Low Frequency Effects — the ".1": a dedicated deep-bass channel for the subwoofer.
Ls / Rs
Left surround / Right surround — the rear speakers.
SMPTE order
Standard channel order in delivered files: L R C LFE Ls Rs. (Film order: L C R Ls Rs LFE.)
Downmix / fold-down
Automatically converting a surround mix to stereo.
Lt/Rt vs Lo/Ro
Lt/Rt encodes surround info so a decoder can rebuild it; Lo/Ro is a plain stereo sum. The delivery spec says which.
Bass management
A playback system re-routing low frequencies to the subwoofer automatically. The LFE buses exist so you control the sub deliberately instead.
48 kHz / 24-bit
Professional sample rate and bit depth for film/TV audio.
Loudness & delivery
LUFS / LKFS
Loudness Units relative to Full Scale — the standard measure of perceived loudness (LKFS is the US name). More negative = quieter.
Integrated
The single loudness number for the whole programme — what specs are judged on.
Short-term / Momentary
Loudness over the last 3 s / 400 ms — for watching while you mix.
LRA (Loudness Range)
How much the loudness varies. Broadcast tolerates less range than cinema.
dBTP / true peak
The real maximum level after digital-to-analogue conversion; specs cap it to prevent distortion.
Dialog-gated
Loudness measured only while speech is present — how Netflix-style services measure.
EBU R128
European broadcast loudness standard: −23 LUFS integrated, −1 dBTP.
ATSC A/85 · CALM Act
US broadcast loudness standard (−24 LKFS, −2 dBTP) and the law that enforces it.
Deliverable
Any file the client contractually requires: masters, stems, M&E…
WAV / AAC / MP3
WAV = uncompressed professional format; AAC/MP3 = compressed web formats (Webmix only).
.drp
A DaVinci Resolve Project export — how you save this template for reuse.
Processing
EQ (Equaliser)
Boosts or cuts chosen frequency ranges — tone control.
Compressor
Automatically turns down loud moments, evening out level. Ratio (3:1) sets how firmly.
Limiter
A brick-wall compressor that stops the signal exceeding a ceiling.
De-esser
Tames harsh "S" sounds in a voice.
Reverb
Simulates a space's echo: room, plate, hall.
Low-pass filter
Lets frequencies below a cutoff through; ~120 Hz on the LFE buses keeps only sub-bass.
Chain FX
Resolve 21: several plugins saved as one reusable preset (up to 6).
FairlightFX
Resolve's built-in suite of audio plugins.
